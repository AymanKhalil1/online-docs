Lumped Damping

Dampers are typically used in structures & machines to minimize the vibrations' effects. Or, in other words, to descrease the maximum amplitude occuring in each cycle.
To model a structure having a damper of a known damping coefficient whose units are force per velocity, ELS has added the "Lumped Damping" feature to Version 9.

Hereunder, we'll show you how to use the "Lumped Damping" feature & observe its effect on a simple pendulum.
Folow the following steps for optimum results :

Step1:Draw a 5 m girder with pinned-free end conditions and duplicate this girder.
Step2:From the "Analysis Settings",choose ground & Boundary Settings and Instead of *Reflecting" , make it *No*. Apply, Ok.
          (This step is to ensure that there is no ground for the pendulum to move freely. )
Step3:And now, let's assign the "Lumped Damping".Select the outermost element of the girder on the right. Double click on "Lumped Damping" in the Preprocessing browser.
           Take the "Lumped Damping" in Z, 10 kg.sec/m.Assign... Apply... Ok.
Step4:Run the solver.
Step5:Open the Results Viewer & wait till the solver is done.
Step6:View the annimation from the front view and observe the two girders.
Step7:To be able to comment on the analysis' results, let's draw an animated chart of the displacement *Z* of the 1st & the 2nd girders.
Step8:Observe the udamped pendulum's curve showing equal peaks,while the damped vibration is dying with time.
Which typically shows the effect of the lumped damping to structures.
