Element Filters

ELS Viewer V10 brings powerful element filtering and viewing capabilities, used to apply filters to specific elements with conditions that are as simple or complex as the user would like. The procedure and capabilities of applying element filters are listed below

Procedure

  1. To open the 'Filtration' dialog window, navigate to the 'Filtration' tab, and double-click the 'Elements' item under Advanced Filter > Filtration.
  2. Click the 'Add' button to add a filter.
  3. To choose the type of active filter to be added, click on the Expand button
  4. In the 'Elements Conditions' dialog box, choose the required element filter condition. The available options are as follows:
    1. General Conditions: by element ID, material, element type, volume, stress/strain, or interface ID.
    2. Coordinates: position in X,Y, and Z
    3. Acceleration: in each degree of freedom or total
    4. Velocity: in each degree of freedom or total
    5. Rotation: around X,Y,Z, or total
    6. Displacement: in X,Y,Z, or total
    7. Force: in X, Y, Z, or total
    8. Moment: around X, Y, Z, or total
    9. Kinetic Energy
    10. Miscellaneous: By Layer, Object Type, Level, Elements Count, or Mesh Type
  5. Click the 'Generate' button, then click anywhere inside the bounds of the sketching area in the 'Filter Details' window to place the active filter condition.
  6. Specify the point in time, or the stage start at which the filter is activated.
  7. Assign a custom color for the filter, or choose to apply the default project colors of element material or type.
  8. Define additional rendering options as required.
  9. Customizing the condition:
    1. To change the values for an added condition, click on the value part of its corresponding block
    2. To cycle through comparison operators (>, <, =, etc.), click on the operator itself
    3. For conditions that implement dropdowns, click on the default value to change it.
  10. To apply multiple conditions, logical operators (AND, OR) can be used.
    1. Add another condition to the sketching area as previously detailed
    2. Click the 'Add Operator Item' button to add a logical operator. Click anywhere in the sketching area to add it. Click on the operator to cycle between the 'AND' and 'OR' operators
    3. To join any two or more conditions with a logical operator, left-click and drag your mouse to draw a connecting line between the yellow square at the top left of the associated condition block to the operator block.
    4. To alter the order of logical operations (to group conditions together), click the 'Add Group Item' button, then click in the sketching area. Then, drag any two or more condition blocks inside the group item block
  11. Click 'Apply' and 'OK' to close out of the 'Filter Details' dialog box
  12. Click 'Apply' to add the cutom filter to the filter list.
  13. To show or hide a filter, check/clear its corresponding checkbox in the 'Filtration' dialog box filter list.
  14. Click 'OK' to close the 'Filtration' window.
