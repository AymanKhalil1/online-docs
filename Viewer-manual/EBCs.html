Elastic Boundary Conditions

In real life, ideal boundary conditions do not always exist and structures are supported by partial motion restrains that we approximate into a pinned or a fixed support. But what if you wanted a better approximation?
In version 9, ELS has made this possible by providing the new feature: Elastic Boundary Conditions which enables you to model any BC in the form of a spring of adjustable stiffness in translation as well as rotation.

Elastic Boundary Conditions


Let’s have a look at this amazing enhancement: We’ll be modeling two pinned-free one dimensional elements one with a support having a rotational stiffness and the other having a support of translational stiffness at its free end.

Step1: Remove the ground to enable a pendulum-like motion
Step2: Draw a pinned-free beam
Step3: Select one of the beam ends
Step4: Convert the loading stage to dynamic and increase the stage duration
Step5: Now assign a pinned restraint
Step6: Replicate the beam to have a total of 3 beams
Step7: Select the free-end of one of them.
Step8: Open the Loading scenario dialog box, choose EBC’s
Step9: Assign a displacement stiffness in the z direction.
Step10: select the pinned end of one of the other beams,
Step11: and follow the same procedures but this time assign rotation stiffness around the Y-direction.
Step12: Run the analysis and open the results’ viewer.
Step13: Run the animation & watch the variation in the 3 beams' motion.
To better observe the differences, draw a chart showing the displacement Z of the outermost element of the 3 beams vs time
As we see, the motion is converted to a semi-restrained semi-free motion which represents most of the realistic structural restraints.
